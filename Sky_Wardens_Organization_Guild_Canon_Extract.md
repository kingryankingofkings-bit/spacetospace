## Core Premise

**Canon:**

An Elan-bodied Drow woman named **Narexi** was cast into and trapped within the **Plane of Negative Energy**. After decades of imprisonment, she escaped, genetically altered by that extended confinement. She now seeks revenge against the **Sky Wardens**, a self-proclaimed and widely accepted world-policing force focused on preventing cruel treatment of civilians.

Narexi despises the Sky Wardens and wants them annihilated. The clash of godly powers has caused tears to other planes to open. At the same time, the **wrought-iron tree of life** has begun overgrowing into the Material Plane, turning entire ecosystems into wrought-iron versions of themselves. These overgrowths disrupt surrounding areas and poison water sources and mana-well points.

## Death and Resurrection

**Canon:**

- SkyWarden bodies teleport to the SkyWardens’ main base in the Land of the Bear immediately upon death, unless interrupted.
- Half-dragons are an exception; they teleport to the Land of the Dragon.
- The existing filled bible states that SkyWardens are instantly transported to the nearest outpost and resurrected.
- Narexi has an ability that cancels the resurrection of a SkyWarden after transport.

**Needs DM Confirmation:**

- `Land of the Bear.txt` says SkyWarden bodies teleport to the SkyWardens’ main base in the Land of the Bear.
- `barely filled storybible.txt` says SkyWardens are transported to nearest outpost and resurrected.
- These may both be true in different contexts, but the exact resurrection routing rule should be locked.

### The Dragon’s Home Bastion

#### Location and Role

**Canon:**

The Sky Warden headquarters for the Land of the Dragon is **The Dragon’s Home Bastion**.

It is overseen by Torinn Rhogar, one of the three Sky Warden leaders/founders.

The Dragon’s Home Bastion is a fortress-citadel.

It is bridged over Chromatic Rapids Run.

The bastion is located roughly halfway between The Iron-Woods and Blood Mountain, about 100 miles east of The Iron-Woods’ eastern edge and about 100 miles west of Blood Mountain.

The lands around the bastion are dense farmland, producing crops and livestock.


**Canon from `ch1-3.md`:**

The bastion sits astride Chromatic Rapids Run on supports of stone and iron-wood.

The river passes through channels beneath the fortress, producing a constant low thunder that can be felt throughout the citadel.

The bastion’s courtyards and processing areas are associated with persistent rime and cold air.


#### Protection Field

**Canon:**

The Dragon’s Home Bastion emits a protection field extending 50 miles in every direction.

This field encompasses nearby farms and townships.

The field does not act as a wall or passive shield.

Instead, any hostile act within the field immediately triggers a forced teleport response.

A designated Sky Warden enforcement party, usually made up of high-ranking Wardens, is teleported directly to the hostile creature’s exact location.

The field responds to all hostile acts.

Hostility is judged by intent and disregard for harm, not by legal category alone.

A starving thief stealing food to feed their family, without desire to harm or exploit others, would not trigger the field.

A thief stealing out of greed, for resale, or with indifference to who suffers from the theft, would count as hostile and trigger a Sky Warden response.

The field determines hostility according to Torinn Rhogar’s Lawful Neutral alignment.

In effect, it judges as though Torinn himself were present and personally assessing the situation.

Torinn personally created this protection field.

Torinn also created the equivalent protection fields at the Bear Bastion and Shadow Bastion.

Most people do not understand the exact mechanics of these bastion fields. Common knowledge is simpler: do not act with hostile intent near a Sky Warden bastion.


**Canon from `ch1-3.md`:**

The field can respond to illegal deep-woods harvesting as **hostile harvest** and can identify the exact hostile location and number of souls involved.

The field can judge mixed intent. A person involved in a hostile act may still show non-hostile or redeemable intent through later action, such as stopping to save another person.

The forced response commonly brings Warden gray-clad enforcement figures in a cold, blue-edged teleportation event.

### Dragon’s Home Bastion Region — Settlements and People

#### Local Demographics

**Canon:**

The protected townships around The Dragon’s Home Bastion have mixed-race populations.

The largest population grouping is human/elf mixed communities.

The second-largest grouping is elves with significant draconic bloodlines.

#### Half-Dragon Social Status

**Canon:**

Across most of Tristul, half-dragons are respected as one of the strongest races.

The major exception is the Hammerforged Isles.

Respect does not mean equality.

Some regions maintain harsh internal hierarchies among half-dragons, often based on the non-dragon half.

In the Land of the Dragon, elf/dragon bloodlines are usually accepted and treated well.

Combinations such as dire wolf/dragon are often looked down on despite great physical strength.

These hierarchies are reinforced by local governing bodies, not by the Sky Wardens.

#### Sky Warden Political Limits

**Canon:**

The Sky Wardens are not a political or ruling organization.

They function as neutral, non-biased defenders.

They usually avoid political interference unless a governing body is committing, enabling, or credibly suspected of acts that harm other beings.

#### Goldweald Export Control Council

**Canon:**

Goldweald’s Iron-wood industry is overseen by the **Goldweald Export Control Council**.

The council consists of 10 merchant council members plus a high-ranking Sky Warden advisor.

The council regulates Iron-wood production, exports, harvest documentation, replanting obligations, and compliance with Sky Warden ecological limits.

The council is politically divided.

Some members accept Sky Warden ecological oversight as necessary.

Others despise the reduction in profits caused by Sky Warden intervention.

The anti-Warden faction is involved in illegal overharvesting deeper inside The Iron-Woods.


**Canon from `ch1-3.md`:**

**Old Bronce** is a named anti-Warden figure associated with the Goldweald export conflict. He argues that the Bastion and tighter Warden quotas are strangling honest trade.

The anti-Warden faction uses silence, silver, and deniability to pay illegal deep-woods harvesters.

### Deep Iron-Woods Western Patrol Incident

**Status:** Canon from `ch4-8.md` / broader mechanics implications need DM confirmation

After Senna Aldweir and Garrow were conscripted, Strike Warden Rak’shamya formed a western patrol to investigate the Iron-Woods dying-note disturbance.

The patrol included Rak’shamya, Brockar, Shae, Billy, Ja’meera, Neesaam, Senna Aldweir, Garrow, and Orla.

The patrol confirmed that the disturbance had escalated from isolated trees ringing the dying note into a spreading chord of multiple Iron-wood trees dying without axes, storms, or ordinary cutting. The disturbance moved **toward the rifts**, not toward roads or legal hauling routes, meaning it was not ordinary poaching.

#### Widened Rift Behavior

The patrol found a widened rift in the deep Iron-Woods. The rift manifested as cold blue nothing-light, leached gray vegetation, cellar-like stale cold, bark opening like a pupil, and accelerated gray death in surrounding Iron-wood.

Normal Iron-Woods rifts leak slowly and can produce Iron-wood treants over long periods. This incident is specifically anomalous: the rift had been widened, causing rapid wrong-waking of treants and fast spread of the dying note.

The rift connects to or opens toward the **Negative Energy Plane**, field-described here as the plane where nothing is and where being is pressured toward non-being.

#### Directed Treants and Rift Feeding

Wrong-woken Iron-wood treants were observed dragging deadfall, gray matter, and Obmils toward the rift, effectively feeding or widening it instead of merely guarding it.

Rak’shamya suspected that something was directing the treants. Because only an Iron-Wild druid can guide Iron-wood treants under existing canon, the current working suspicion is that an Iron-Wild druid is being reached, made, worn, or otherwise exploited from the far side of the rift.

**Important limitation:** No specific Iron-Wild druid has been identified. Do not name the directing agent until the DM establishes it.

#### Far-Side Entity

Something pale, patient, and ancient was perceived leaning or watching through the widened rift. Its identity is unestablished. Do not automatically identify it as Narexi, Cryonax, a deity, or any existing villain until the DM confirms it.

The entity appears to have learned that SkyWardens use death-transport / resurrection routing as a back door against death.

#### Rift Status After Senna’s Intervention

Senna used Tithe and willed magic near the widened rift to force the opening to narrow. She did **not** close the rift permanently. It remains half-closed / narrowed / leaned-on, not sealed.

#### Non-Merged Aura Note

`ch4-8.md` continues depicting a stationary two-mile frost-line around The Dragon’s Home Bastion. This remains unresolved because existing locked canon says Torinn’s aura keeps the air within two miles of Torinn at 31°F. Do not lock the stationary frost-line until the DM confirms whether it is a separate Bastion effect, a residue of Torinn’s aura, or a chapter-draft adjustment.

## SkyWarden Recruitment, Conscription, and Resurrection Tattoos

**Canon / DM-Established Update:**

The **SkyWardens are a guild**.

The majority of SkyWarden members are **former criminals** who were given a chance at rehabilitation through service.

Many were offered the choice between joining the SkyWardens or facing severe punishment such as losing their hands, execution, or life imprisonment.

Upon conscription into the guild, every SkyWarden receives a **magical tattoo**.

The tattoo grants random, very low-level abilities.

The tattoo also grants one special death-response function: if a SkyWarden is slain, the body is immediately teleported to the nearest SkyWarden bastion, where an automatic resurrection ability activates and brings the SkyWarden back to life.


### Dragon’s Home Bastion Recruit Intake Details

**Canon from `ch1-3.md`:**

New conscripts may be brought into The Dragon’s Home Bastion by prison-cart and processed beneath or within the fortress before being formally entered into service.

The offer of SkyWarden service is often called mercy, but it is usually not a true choice. The alternatives can include the loss of a hand, loss of years through imprisonment, execution, or other severe punishment depending on the crime and region.

For regulated iron-wood crimes in the Land of the Dragon, the clerked charge can be framed as theft against the protected commons.

A recruit’s service is described as a debt to the commons paid through work the commons most needs.

The rank distinction matters: new conscripts may be members of the SkyWarden guild, but in field hierarchy they begin as **Wardens**, the lowest rank, not as high-title **Sky Wardens**.

### Tattoo Marking Procedure and Edge Cases

**Canon from `ch1-3.md`:**

The tattoo is applied by a specialist known as **the Inker**, who is not necessarily a Warden.

The mark draws its own design rather than being chosen by the recruit or fully designed by the Inker.

The gift granted by the mark may be small, redundant with an existing talent, or not obviously useful.

Inkers study the design as it forms and may be able to read broad implications from the mark, though this interpretive practice is not established as public doctrine.

A broken-ring tattoo design can **catch** if the death-transport or resurrection route ever snags. This is a foreshadowed edge case compatible with Narexi’s resurrection-cancel threat, but the exact mechanics remain unconfirmed.

**Known example:** Senna Aldweir’s mark is a thin gray ring with a single broken link, like a chain opened once and never fully closed. Its gift improved her existing dark-sight.

## SkyWarden Oath, Recruit Training, and Field Resurrection Observations

New recruits at The Dragon’s Home Bastion are taught the Warden oath phrase by phrase.

The oath frames conscription as a debt to the commons and death as something that does not end service.

Ha’ashana, dwarf Strike Warden and paladin / marshal, serves as drill-warden during oath training.

Willing casters train separately in a smaller yard behind a door that requires a Warden’s word to open. They are taught to understand their cost before being taught what their will can do.

### Healing and Lasting Consequence

Brockar, a human Warden-cleric, healed Garrow’s ruined ankle after the Iron-wood assassin vine injury. The bone knitted quickly, but Garrow retained a limp. This establishes that Warden healing can be powerful and practical without erasing every lasting consequence of injury.

During the western patrol incident, Ja’meera lost a hand to an Obmil/void-cold event, died, and was successfully routed by his Warden mark to the Bastion. He later returned whole, with both hands restored and no memory of losing the hand.

**Needs DM Confirmation:** Confirm whether removal of death-wound memory is standard, case-specific, or tied to void / Negative Energy injury.

### Observed Failed Resurrection Route: Garrow Died Wrong

Garrow died near the widened deep Iron-Woods rift after being struck by a wrong-woken treant. The death-transport began, but the route snagged near the open Negative Energy rift and failed. His body did not teleport to the Bastion, and he did not resurrect.

**Important limitation:** Do not treat this as a universal failure mode for all Warden deaths. It occurred near a widened Negative Energy rift where the world was thin or absent. The exact general mechanics remain **Needs DM Confirmation**.

## Senna Aldweir

**Status:** Canon from `ch1-3.md`  
**Origin:** Goldweald, Land of the Dragon.  
**Age:** Twenty-three.  
**Class / Build Note:** The chapter compliance note identifies Senna as **Rogue / Hexblade level 4**.  
**Role:** Opening-story viewpoint character; conscripted Warden candidate.

### Background

Senna is the daughter of an Iron-wood replanter from Goldweald.

She learned the cost of iron-wood labor as a child by watching her mother work the sapling rows under Export Control Council oversight.

By adulthood, Senna stopped planting Iron-wood and began stealing it. She considered poaching a better-paying, slower form of ruin than honest replanting labor.

She worked under Garrow, who taught her deep-woods survival, illegal harvesting habits, and how to lie to people with ledgers.

### Bloodline and Senses

Senna carries faint old draconic or winged ancestry.

A pale streak of scale runs along her jaw.

Before receiving her Warden mark, this old blood already gave her limited ability to see in very deep darkness.

After receiving her tattoo, her dark-sight improved.

### Magic

Senna can cast by willing magic into being rather than using conventional components.

Her known cost is memory loss.

Her use of Tithe to curse an Iron-wood assassin vine cost her the memory of her mother singing while planting.

### Warden Mark

Senna’s Warden tattoo is a thin gray ring with one broken link, like a chain opened once and never properly closed.

The Inker warns that a broken ring may catch if the death-transport or resurrection door ever snags.

This creates a personal connection between Senna and the broader SkyWarden resurrection-interruption threat.

### Gear

Senna carries **Tithe**, a long single-edged knife carved from a broken Iron-wood treant limb.

Tithe’s haft remains faintly warm with planar energy and can channel Senna’s willed curse magic.
### Western Patrol Outcome

In the western patrol incident, Senna saw the widened deep-west rift, Obmil escalation, Ja’meera’s successful resurrection-routing, and Garrow’s failed death-transport.

Senna used Tithe near the open rift to force the rift to narrow. She did not seal it permanently. The cost was immediate and severe: she lost the memory of the night her mother died. The grief remained, but the memory-reason was gone.

After this act, Tithe went cold for the first recorded time.

Senna understands that her broken-ring mark may be more vulnerable to resurrection-route snags than a normal whole mark, especially near Negative Energy rift influence.

Senna also appears able, through faint draconic blood, to perceive Constellation Dragons in certain star-clusters. The generality of this ability is not confirmed.

### SkyWarden Death Culture and “Died Wrong” Aftermath

**Status:** Manuscript-established / compatible with prior bible canon.

After Garrow’s failed death-route, the Dragon’s Home Bastion experiences a doctrinal shock because normal SkyWarden culture assumes that death is temporary service debt rather than a permanent ending.

Manuscript-established rites and customs:

- A recruit’s first successful death-and-return may be acknowledged with a hot cup and the word **owe**.
- A Warden’s tenth death may be marked with a small black mark beside the conscription mark and drinks bought by the rank.
- Before Garrow’s case, the Wardens appear to lack a formal rite for a Warden who dies and does not return.
- Brockar improvises or articulates the principle: **a Warden gets carried home, or a Warden gets buried by his rank.**
- Garrow’s body remains in the deep west until a return mission buries him.
- Garrow is buried with his hatchet.
- Senna says **owe** over Garrow even though he will not rise to repay it.

### Resurrection-Route Snag Pattern

**Status:** Manuscript-established plot pattern / major mechanics need DM confirmation.

Chapters 9–13 expand Garrow’s “died wrong” event into a larger pattern:

- The Wardens audit other failed or questionable death-routing events after the deep-west incident.
- Snags cluster near places relevant to SkyWarden routing and bastions.
- Reported clusters include:
  - The Rotted Marsh region / Land of the Shadow.
  - The Deathsand coast.
  - The edge of the Land of the Bear’s mainland near the main base.
  - The deep Iron-Woods / Dragon’s Home Bastion region.
- Manuscript logic states that Narexi’s agents are opening Black Cursed doors near bastion routes so that Warden death-carrying can be interrupted.
- The goal is not simply to kill Wardens, but to make SkyWardens doubt the one promise their service is built around: **when I fall, I will rise.**
- The tactic feeds the public legitimacy crisis by making the Wardens appear mortal, frightened, leashed, and unreliable.

**Limitation:** Keep this as story-arc canon unless DM confirms exact routing mechanics. The bible’s older main-base / nearest-outpost / half-dragon routing question still needs reconciliation.
