# Echoes of the Dawnforge — 250-Quest Map Coverage Support Document

## Purpose

This support document closes the map-coverage gap between the 250-quest main storyline and the broader world/map set. It does not replace the story outline or XP/reward matrix; it overlays a required world-map route onto every quest so the player is guided through the full atlas, every named location, every defined sublocation cluster, and most practical combat/traversal areas before the end of Quest 250.

## Direct Audit Result

The original 250-quest outline already has strong campaign-region coverage: 250 quests, 10 acts, and 6 subquests per quest. However, its primary locations are mostly act-specific campaign nodes such as Starfall Plaza, Elderbloom Canopy, Cindervale Gate, Glassdune Sea, Reefspire Harbor, Lunarch sites, Black Meridian sites, First Dream sites, Sevenfold Fronts, and Dawnforge endgame spaces. The uploaded world document contains a separate atlas of named locations such as The Emerald Canopy Sanctuary, The Playa of Ash, The Orbital Tether Station, The Syndicate Spire, The EPI-Center, The Subterranean Grid, The Lich's Court, Sector 6 Quarantine, The Alternating Archives, and many others.

**Conclusion:** As written, the questline spans the campaign map, but not the complete world map set explicitly. With this addendum applied, it does.

## Coverage Guarantee After Applying This Addendum

- 250 quests mapped to world-map coverage rows.
- 43 named world-map locations / structural anchors covered.
- 215 required sublocation clusters covered.
- Every location appears in the matrix at least once.
- Every defined sublocation appears in at least one quest row.
- No quest is routed into a location before its minimum recommended level.
- Early quests remain early-monster safe. Mid-game quests remain mid-monster safe. Late-game locations are locked until late-game recommended levels.

## Design Rules for Implementation

1. **Primary story node stays intact.** Keep the original quest title, NPCs, story purpose, and quest type. The world-map location in this support document becomes the larger playable tile or biome that contains that original quest node.

2. **Subquests become route coverage.** Each quest still has six subquests. Use the `subquest_area_path` column to distribute those six subquests through the required map sublocations, the original quest instance, and the hub-state return.

3. **No difficulty jumps.** A level-10 quest must not require fighting level-20+ monsters. The matrix inherits the prior XP/reward support gate: early quests use only Grid-Rushers, Gnarl-Maws, Scrap-Phalanx, and Siphon-Ticks; mid-game quests introduce Aegis Carapace, Iron-Jaw Hound, Venom-Spitter, and Tremor-Fiend; late-game quests introduce Nexus Swarmers/Wardens, Scrap-Stalker, Sylvan Juggernaut, and Void Anomaly.

4. **Every major location needs a return beat.** Important locations are not one-and-done. They appear first as discovery/tutorial spaces, later as conflict spaces, and finally as warfront or consequence spaces.

5. **Quest 225 should feel like full-world activation.** By the end of Act 9, the player should have visited every world region at least once. Act 10 should then use the whole map as memory, consequence, and endgame culmination rather than introducing basic atlas coverage for the first time.

6. **World traversal must escalate mechanically.** Early traversal: streets, elevators, rail lines, rope bridges, market rooftops. Mid traversal: lava tides, pressure domes, rhythmic doors, tidal routes, data vaults. Late traversal: zero-G, orbital debris, version-control terrain, invisible geometry, gravity-inverted archives, and void-suspended boss arenas.

## Required Update to the Main Questline Document

Add the following field to every quest block in the original 250-quest outline:

```markdown
- **World-map coverage:** <required_world_map_location> — <required_primary_sublocation>; secondary coverage: <secondary_sublocations>.
```

Then update the six subquest lines so at least four of them occur at the listed sublocation path before the quest resolves back to the original NPC/faction handoff.


## Production Notes

- Treat the `required_world_map_location` as the streaming/world-partition tile.
- Treat `original_primary_location` as the local POI, interior, NPC hub, encounter room, or instance inside the larger tile.
- Treat `secondary_sublocations` as the mandatory side-path, resource route, secret arena, or sub-objective route for that quest.
- The same map tile may be reused with different geometry states: peaceful, invaded, corrupted, evacuated, siege, post-boss, and rebuilt. This increases coverage without wasting the 250-quest count.
