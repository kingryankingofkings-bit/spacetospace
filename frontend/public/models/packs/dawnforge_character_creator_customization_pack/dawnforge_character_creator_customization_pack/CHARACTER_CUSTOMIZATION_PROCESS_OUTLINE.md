# Echoes of the Dawnforge — Character Creation & Deep Customization Process Outline

**Document purpose:** Define an extensive, production-ready character creation flow for a high-end online 3D action RPG/MMO. The creator should support deep identity, body, face, hair, skin, markings, gear, class-fantasy, animation, accessibility, and multiplayer-readability choices without granting unfair combat advantages.

**Source alignment:** The storyline frames the player as a Wayfinder in a large online 3D action RPG/MMO campaign with world-state hubs, factions, solo and group content, and live-service expansion hooks. The customization system should therefore read well in cinematics, hubs, dungeons, raids, PvP-style silhouettes, and long-term social spaces. The world document also defines strong location identities, class fantasies, and biome aesthetics that should feed regional origin cosmetics, class imprints, tattoos, scars, materials, and preview environments.

## 1. Design Pillars

1. **Player identity first:** Gender, body, voice, pronouns, facial structure, hair, and style must be independent systems. Presets are starting points, not locks.
2. **Deep but readable:** The character must look unique up close while remaining readable in combat at MMO camera distance.
3. **Cosmetic fairness:** Height, weight, body width, hairstyles, prosthetics, and accessories must not alter server hitboxes, targeting fairness, or animation advantage.
4. **World-authentic variety:** Every major region and class fantasy should have visual hooks in the creator: celestial, industrial, verdant, resonant, oceanic, lunar, data-glass, desert, dream, and scrap-tech aesthetics.
5. **Representation without caricature:** Natural hair, darker skin tones, scars, vitiligo, disabilities, mature faces, prosthetics, body size, and gender-diverse options are core creator features, not premium add-ons.
6. **Modular production:** All options should be data-driven, tagged by body rig compatibility, material set, color mask, physics profile, clipping risk, and unlock state.
7. **Live-service safe:** The system must support future hairstyles, tattoos, prosthetics, event cosmetics, class skins, and faction styles without rebuilding the avatar pipeline.

## 2. Character Creation Flow

### 00. Entry Screen & Creator Contract
- Show the player a rotating hero shot in neutral lighting.
- Explain that appearance choices are cosmetic and can be changed later unless specifically marked as story-origin choices.
- Provide Randomize, Guided Randomize, Reset, Undo, Redo, Save Draft, and Compare Previous buttons.
- Offer low-motion UI mode, high-contrast UI, screen-reader labels, and colorblind-safe preview filters.

### 01. Identity Layer
- Select name and optional surname/callsign.
- Select pronouns: he/him, she/her, they/them, ze/zir, custom, hidden, or region-localized variants.
- Select gender identity label: man, woman, nonbinary, agender, genderfluid, custom, or undisclosed. This affects only UI reference and dialogue text, not available appearance parts.
- Select form of address: Wayfinder, Captain, Adept, Warden, Pilot, Savant, Handler, Striker, Weaver, or custom role title.
- Select voice family independently of gender and body.

### 02. Body Foundation
- Choose body model baseline: light, average, heavy, athletic, broad, lean, soft, compact, towering, and custom sculpt.
- Select presentation preset only as a starting point: masculine, feminine, androgynous, heroic, elder, youthful, rugged, elegant, synthetic, verdant, lunar, or custom.
- Adjust height, body mass, muscle, fat distribution, shoulders, chest, waist, hips, arms, legs, hands, feet, neck, posture, gait, and stance.
- Show server hitbox overlay to make clear that appearance does not change combat collision.

### 03. Origin / Cultural Style
- Choose regional origin for cosmetic motifs and starting dialogue flavor.
- Origin affects default clothing, scars, tattoos, jewelry, hair accessories, and hub recognition lines; it should not lock classes or power.
- Allow multi-origin blending for mixed identity: primary origin, secondary influence, and adopted faction.

### 04. Skin & Surface Detail
- Select natural or fantasy skin tone, undertone, texture, pores, oiliness, dryness, scars, moles, freckles, vitiligo, birthmarks, sun exposure, age lines, and body hair.
- Preview skin under daylight, dark dungeon, neon city, underwater, zero-G, desert glare, and cinematic close-up lighting.
- Let players separately tune face, body, hands, prosthetic seam zones, and scar coloration.

### 05. Face Sculpt
- Pick one of 60 baseline face presets across masculine, feminine, and androgynous/nonbinary presentation sets.
- Use detailed sliders for skull shape, forehead, brow, eyes, nose, cheeks, mouth, jaw, chin, ears, teeth, asymmetry, and age lines.
- Allow left/right asymmetry controls for eyes, scars, brows, ears, cheek height, and jaw tilt.

### 06. Eyes, Brows, Teeth, and Expression
- Customize iris, sclera, pupil shape, heterochromia, blind eye, ocular implants, eyelashes, eyebrows, under-eye texture, teeth shape, fang/canine options, smile style, and default expression.
- Class preview should show how eye glow reacts to Protocol, Flux, Resonant, Void, Sylvan, or Orbital effects.

### 07. Hair, Scalp, and Facial Hair
- Choose from 45 starter hairstyles: 15 masculine-presentation, 15 feminine-presentation, and 15 androgynous/nonbinary-presentation presets. Every hairstyle remains selectable by every character.
- Tune length, volume, curls, coils, parting, frizz, shine, hairline, shaved patterns, physics intensity, headwear compatibility, primary color, secondary color, streaks, ombre, glow, and metallic dye.
- Choose facial hair from 20 styles, with density, length, curl, color, beads, braid, scar interruption, and age-grey controls.

### 08. Marks, Scars, Tattoos, and Body Story
- Add scars by type, age, color, raisedness, and location: face, scalp, torso, back, arms, hands, legs, prosthetic seams, and aether-core impact zones.
- Add tattoos and war paint using region sets, class sets, geometric sets, ancestral sets, biome sets, abstract sets, and player-custom color channels.
- Add blemishes, acne, pores, moles, burn marks, ritual marks, healed fractures, surgical seams, stretch marks, and calluses.

### 09. Augmentations, Prosthetics, and Accessibility Features
- Offer cosmetic prosthetic arms, hands, legs, feet, ocular implants, hearing devices, neural ports, spinal braces, breathing masks, mobility braces, burn compression garments, and cybernetic seams.
- Make assistive devices visually heroic and world-integrated while avoiding stat penalties or stereotypes.
- Allow prosthetic material themes: wood/bark, brass, scrap-steel, glass, hard-light, lunar ceramic, coral, black void metal, and Dawnforge gold.

### 10. Accessories & Personal Items
- Choose jewelry, piercings, goggles, masks, scarves, charms, belts, satchels, prayer cords, tool rigs, back trophies, hair beads, earrings, nose rings, lip rings, brow rings, glasses, respirators, and class trinkets.
- Preview clipping against helmets, hoods, hair, collars, and starter gear.

### 11. Class Imprint
- Pick class and preview class-specific idle, weapon draw, combat aura, movement, visual effects, and interface meter.
- Class imprint is cosmetic at creation: tattoos, aura color, weapon skin preview, stance, intro animation, and class trainer dialogue.
- Allow class cosmetics to be disabled for players who prefer clean silhouettes.

### 12. Voice, Personality, and Animation
- Select voice family, pitch, grit, resonance, accent style, combat-callout frequency, pain response, laugh, greeting, victory line, and emote tone.
- Select walk, run, sprint, idle, combat-ready posture, seated pose, social emotes, victory pose, defeat recovery, and group-intro stance.
- Provide low-frequency comfort toggle for players sensitive to repetitive voice barks.

### 13. Starter Gear and Color Palette
- Choose starter underlayer, armor silhouette, material wear, boots, gloves, belt, cloak/cape/no-cape, and helmet visibility.
- Pick palette slots for cloth, metal, leather, hard-light, trim, glow, class energy, and dirt/wear.
- Gear is cosmetic during creation and can be replaced by progression rewards later.

### 14. Preview Lab
- Preview avatar in six contexts: dialogue close-up, hub idle, sprint traversal, class attack, mount/vehicle stance, and four-player party lineup.
- Show lighting modes: Meridian daylight, Elderbloom green light, Cindervale forge heat, Pelagos underwater, Lunarch low gravity, Syndicate neon, First Dream surreal, and Dawnforge final light.
- Show silhouette-readability score and clipping warnings.

### 15. Final Review & Launch
- Display full summary: identity, origin, body, face, hair, markings, class, voice, animations, and starter gear.
- Run automated validation: missing name, prohibited name, severe clipping, unreadable skin/tattoo contrast, hidden eyes in cutscenes, hair/headwear conflict, voice mismatch warning if player enabled warnings.
- Save creation code, appearance seed, and editable draft. Play intro cinematic after confirmation.

- **Weight / body mass:** thin, average, heavy, fat, soft, dense, and compact variations.

- **Fat distribution:** belly, waist, hips, thighs, arms, chest, back, face.
- **Shoulder width:** narrow to broad.

- **Waist shape:** straight, tapered, soft, broad, narrow.
- **Hip width:** narrow to wide.
- **Arm length:** short to long; animation retargeted.
- **Leg length:** short to long; armor length retargeted.
- **Hand size:** small to large; weapon grip corrected.
- **Foot size:** small to large; boot mesh corrected.
- **Neck length:** short to long; collar clipping check.
- **Posture:** upright, relaxed, hunched, proud, guarded, monk-like, pilot-ready, heavy-braced.
- **Gait:** steady, swagger, restrained, elegant, military, floating, heavy, feral, soft-footed.

## 18. Randomization System

Randomization should help players discover combinations without producing offensive, unreadable, or broken avatars.

- **Full Random:** randomizes all cosmetic fields except identity, pronouns, and name unless explicitly enabled.
- **Guided Random:** locks selected categories and randomizes the rest.
- **Region-Coherent Random:** generates a character that visually fits one or more selected origins.
- **Class-Coherent Random:** generates tattoos, gear, colors, and pose around one selected class.
- **Realistic Random:** uses natural tones, grounded hair colors, and low fantasy effects.
- **High-Fantasy Random:** allows extreme skin tones, glowing eyes, aether scars, animated tattoos, and implants.
- **Family Resemblance Random:** uses a saved character as a genetic/style seed for related NPCs or alts.
- **Streamer-Safe Random:** blocks copyrighted-looking emblems, offensive names, and high-flash effects.

## 20. Technical Implementation Requirements

- **Data model:** Every option uses stable IDs, tags, dependencies, rarity, unlock source, material channels, body compatibility, and localization keys.
- **Rigging:** All bodies share compatible skeletons; extreme height and body-mass changes use retargeted animation without changing combat capsules.
- **Blendshapes:** Face sliders are blendshape-driven with clamp ranges to avoid broken lip sync, eye clipping, and helmet intersection.
- **Hair:** Hair assets include LODs, physics profiles, headwear modes, wetness response, helmet tuck meshes, and fallback low-performance meshes.
- **Skin shaders:** Support melanin-rich tones, subsurface variation, scars, tattoos, fantasy glow, vitiligo, pores, dirt, bloodless combat wear, and environmental wetness.
- **Network replication:** Only final selected asset IDs, slider values, and color channels are replicated; heavy mesh data is client-resolved.
- **Performance:** Creator assets must pass hub stress testing with many players visible; animated hair and glow effects must have distance-based LOD fallbacks.
- **Moderation:** Names, custom text tattoos, emblem uploads, and public appearance codes need moderation tooling.
- **Accessibility:** Creator supports screen reader labels, colorblind filters, low motion, large UI, high contrast, camera zoom, and input remapping.
- **Save system:** Save appearance codes, drafts, favorites, before/after comparisons, and import/export codes.
- **QA automation:** Run clipping tests, lighting tests, animation tests, dark-skin tattoo contrast tests, headwear tests, facial hair tests, prosthetic animation tests, and cutscene lip-sync tests.
